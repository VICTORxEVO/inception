Architecture Features

Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

Where each architecture makes different design choices and trade-offs.

Compute

RDNA 4 — Dual SIMD32

64 shader processors per CU split across two SIMD32 units. Improved out-of-order scheduler issues up to 4 independent instructions per SIMD per clock. Better divergence handling for mixed game/compute workloads.

Blackwell — SIMT + FP4 Units

128 FP32 CUDA cores per SM. Blackwell adds native FP4 and FP6 precision for AI inference alongside FP32/FP16 traditional compute. Higher raw shader count favours brute-force rasterisation scenarios.

Ray Tracing

RDNA 4 — 3rd-Gen Ray Accelerators

One hardware Ray Accelerator per CU — 64 total on Navi 48. 2× BVH traversal throughput versus RDNA 3. Hardware handles procedural intersections, reducing shader-processor load during complex RT scenes.

Blackwell — 5th-Gen RT Cores

Two dedicated RT Cores per SM — 140 total on GB203. Blackwell RT Cores include a dedicated SER (Shader Execution Reordering) unit inside the RT pipeline, improving occupancy for highly divergent RT workloads. Generally leads in raw RT performance.

AI & Upscaling

RDNA 4 — WMMA Cores + FSR 4

New dedicated WMMA matrix units support FP16, BF16, INT8, and INT4. Enables FSR 4, AMD's first machine-learning upscaler. Runs independently of the shader processors. 4× improvement vs RDNA 3 in AI throughput.

Blackwell — 4th-Gen Tensor + DLSS 4 MFG

4th-gen Tensor Cores support FP4 precision (first time in a consumer GPU), providing far higher AI throughput for inference. DLSS 4 adds Multi-Frame Generation — generating up to 3 frames per rendered frame — offering massive effective frame rate gains.

Memory Subsystem

RDNA 4 — GDDR6 + Infinity Cache

16 GB GDDR6 at 640 GB/s augmented by 64 MB of on-die Infinity Cache. The cache's hit rate dramatically reduces effective latency, compensating for lower raw memory bandwidth versus GDDR7.

Blackwell — GDDR7

16 GB GDDR7 delivers 896 GB/s of raw memory bandwidth — 40% more than Navi 48. No large on-die cache equivalent; Blackwell relies on raw bandwidth and a 48 MB L2 for latency reduction.

Summary

Which Architecture Wins?

It depends on what you prioritise.

💰

Best Value: RDNA 4

At $599, the RX 9070 XT delivers ~93% of RTX 5070 Ti performance for $150 less. For pure rasterisation value, RDNA 4 wins clearly.

🌟

Best Ray Tracing: Blackwell

NVIDIA's 5th-gen RT Cores and SER give a significant lead in ray-traced titles. If RT is your priority, the RTX 5070 Ti or RTX 5070 hold the advantage.

🤖

Best AI Features: Blackwell

DLSS 4 Multi-Frame Generation is a meaningful advantage for supported titles, generating up to 3 extra frames. FSR 4 on RDNA 4 is competitive but lacks MFG.

DLSS 4 MFG
⚡

Efficiency: RDNA 4

304 W TBP on Navi 48 delivers competitive rasterisation perf-per-watt. GB203 at 300 W offers more raw throughput but at a higher cost.

Better $/perf

Bottom Line

RDNA 4 is the smarter buy for rasterisation gaming at the $599 price point. Blackwell's Blackwell architecture holds architectural leads in ray tracing and AI features — particularly DLSS 4 MFG — making it the choice for content creators, ray-tracing enthusiasts, and those on platforms that support Multi-Frame Generation.
